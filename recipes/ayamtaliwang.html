Ayam Taliwang

Taliwang chicken is a type of spicy grilled chicken originating from an area called Taliwang in Sumbawa, West Nusa Tenggara. In the area of origin, Taliwang Chicken is usually served with Plecing Kangkung and Beberuk Eggplant as a side dish.

The appearance of Taliwang Chicken can be said to be similar to grilled chicken in general, but Taliwang Chicken has a distinctive aroma of lime leaves which are used in the seasoning for its ungkep. It's very easy to make, it only takes a few simple ingredients and takes less than an hour.

Resources that can help to make dishes (RasaBunda)

Ingredients

  • Whole breasted chicken without breaking - 1 tail
  • Lime - 1 piece
  • Water - 500 ml
  • Oil for frying - 3 tbsp

    • Ground spices:

  • Curly red chilies - 10 pcs
  • Red cayenne pepper - 10 pieces
  • Shallots - 12 grains
  • Garlic - 6 cloves
  • Pecan - 4 eggs
  • Kencur - 5 cm
  • Red tomatoes - 1 pc
  • Terasi - 1 tsp

    • Other Seasonings:

  • Salt - 1/2 tbsp
  • Sweet soy sauce - 1 tbsp
  • Brown sugar - 1 tbsp
  • Mushroom broth - 1 tsp
  • Orange leaves - 5 pieces

Steps Making Ayam Taliwang

  1. Marinate the chicken with lime juice and let it sit for a while. Wash clean then drain.
  2. Heat the oil then saute ground spices with lime leaves until fragrant then add water.
  3. Add salt, sweet soy sauce, brown sugar and mushroom broth. Cook until the water boils.
  4. Add the chicken and simmer over low heat until the water is reduced and the chicken is tender.
  5. Grill the chicken while occasionally basting the remaining ungkepan seasoning.
  6. Serve the Taliwang Chicken with the remaining chicken ungkepan seasoning, kangkung plecing, and beberuk chili sauce.

Note

  1. You can use coals, grill pan, or oven to roast the chicken.
  2. Chicken can be cut into pieces according to taste (it doesn't have to be whole).
